Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Data Frame ("we," "us," "our," or "Company"). Data Frame is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our platform.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect information about you in a variety of ways. The information we may collect on the site includes:

  • Personal Data: Depending on how you interact with our service, we may collect personal information such as your name and email address if you voluntarily provide it.
  • Project Data: Information you create while using Data Frame (database schemas, field definitions, etc.) is stored locally in your browser by default.
  • Usage Data: We may collect information about how you interact with the platform, including the features you use and actions you take.
  • Device Information: We may collect information about the device you use to access Data Frame, including browser type, operating system, and IP address.

3. Use of Your Information

Having accurate information about you permits us to provide you with a smooth, efficient, and customized experience. Specifically, we may use information collected about you via the site to:

  • Improve the website and platform's functionality
  • Generate and analyze usage statistics
  • Troubleshoot and resolve technical issues
  • Respond to your inquiries and provide customer support
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Enhance platform security and prevent fraud

4. Disclosure of Your Information

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may disclose your information when required by law or when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

5. Data Storage and Security

Data Frame prioritizes the security of your information. By default, your project data is stored locally in your browser's local storage and is not transmitted to our servers. If you choose to use cloud synchronization features (if available), your data will be encrypted during transmission.

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We may use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience. These technologies help us understand how you use the platform and may remember your preferences. You can control cookie settings through your browser.

7. Third-Party Links

The Data Frame platform may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

8. Children's Privacy

Data Frame does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to delete such information and terminate the child's account.

9. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

  • Access, update, or delete your personal information
  • Opt-out of receiving marketing communications
  • Request information about what data we have collected
  • Export your project data from the platform

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, and other factors. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date above.

11. Contact Us

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us through the Data Frame platform or submit your inquiries to the address provided in our Terms of Service.

Data Frame is committed to complying with applicable privacy laws and regulations, including GDPR, CCPA, and other similar regulations. If you are a resident of the EU, GDPR rights apply to your personal data.
